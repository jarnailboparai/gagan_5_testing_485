Mare Island and the Ferry Terminal

Across the Napa River lies Mare Island. Mare Island was the site of the first U.S. Navy base on the Pacific Coast, established in 1854 by Capt. David G. Farragut. During its 142 year history, Mare Island Naval Shipyard built more than
500 ships for the U.S. Navy. Since its closing in 1996, Mare Island has been revitalized under the auspices of Lennar Mare Island.

Vallejo Ferry Terminal
For most of its history Vallejo has had direct ferry service with San Francisco. Ferries also carried passengers to Mare Island and other parts of the bay. One hundred years ago ferry passengers could disembark at the Vallejo waterfront and then take an electric interurban train as far north as Calistoga.
