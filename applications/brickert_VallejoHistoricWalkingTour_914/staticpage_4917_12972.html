Lower Georgia Street

200 Block of Georgia Street
As the busy main street of a prosperous navy town, lower Georgia Street once had a notorious reputation for its many taverns, gaming parlors, and other businesses catering to the needs of adventurous sailors. This colorful part of Vallejo was torn down in the early 1960s. City Hall, the John F. Kennedy Library, and other civic buildings are now located in
this area.

Old Town Mural 
This 9’ by 96’ mural was designed by local artist Miro Salazar and depicts four distinct features of Vallejo. The first panel depicts Mare island history. The second shows the waterfront, ferry, and community activity. The third panel depicts our downtown and farmer’s market. The fourth panel depicts our architectural heritage.
