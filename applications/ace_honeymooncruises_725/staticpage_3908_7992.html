So perhaps youíd like to try out Tom Cruiseís cliff-hanging, mountain climbing experience in the Grand Canyon. Or perhaps go skiing in the mountains. How about a trip to the beach? Why not go cruising? 

Picture this: secluded beaches, dining in balconies overlooking the ocean, gourmet delight, star-gazing, musicals, historic visits, island excursions, sleeping in one place and waking up in a new one. With cruising, you get to try out all these and more, all in one trip. And donít worry about getting seasick. With all the things a perfect get-away cruise offers you, youíd hardly think about the water. 

The best place to start? Mexico. Known for its colorful culture, verdant rain forests, stunning architecture and most of all, its paradise beaches, Mexico offers a wonderful promise of adventure and relaxation. 

There are two types of Mexico cruises you can avail of. The Baja Mexico Cruise offers you a three to four night cruise from Los Angeles or San Diego. It crosses the California coast to Mexicoís Baja Peninsula. It has four ports of call: San Diego, CA, Catalina Island, CA, Ensenado, Mexico and Cabo San Lucas, Mexico. On the other hand, the Mexican Riviera Cruise is a seven to ten night cruise get-away. Departure points are Los Angeles, San Diego and San Francisco. The experience offers you a visit to one or several ports of call from the Baja cruise, as well as stop-overs in the ports of Mazatlan, Puerto Vallarta, Manzanillo, Ixtapa and Acapulco. 

What To See 

Among the different tourist destinations in Mexico, its beach resorts command the highest attention. 

Acapulco. Visitors can enjoy the islandís golden sand, waves and palm trees, not to mention sunsets at Pie de la Cuesta beach. It also offers water skiing and fresh water lagoons at ìCovucaí or ìTres Palosî. 

Catalina. A mix of valleys, coves, beaches, and mountains, Catalina is home to thousands of native flora and fauna, such as the Catalina Mahogany, the Catalina Island Fox, and the Beechey Ground Squirrel. 

Manzanillo. Known as the ìSailfish World Capitalî because of the world record sailfish caught here, Manzanillo is ideal for deep sea fishing and other types of water sports. It has incredible coral reefs and a breathtaking underwater resource that any diver or snorkeler would surely love. 

Puerto Vallarta. This is the place to go for honeymoons, weddings, as well as meetings and conventions. It boosts of the surf, sand, and sun, and other offerings to its visitors like, sport fishing, golf, motorcycling, whale watching, art, diving, hot air balloons, and gourmet food. Talk about everything in one place! 
Ixtapa. Ixtapa is a quaint, small fishing town, with its cobblestones-paved streets and tropical look architecture. It has restaurants, boutiques, jewelry stores, hotels and tourist markets that youíd want to explore and splurge your money on. 
Cruising

Mexico cruises are year-round. You can go on a cruise as part of a western Caribbean trip or as as full Mexican exploration. It is best to schedule your trip during the coutnryís dry season, from November to May. The best deals, however, are available during the fall. 

Spice up your vacation with some Mexican flavor. A weekís sailing across the Mexican coast is just what you need in your stress-filled lifestyle. Afterall, nothing beats a cruise!
